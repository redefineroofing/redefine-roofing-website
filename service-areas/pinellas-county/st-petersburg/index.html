The Sunshine City Is Hard on Roofs

St. Petersburg's famous sunshine — the city once ran a promotion giving away newspapers on days the sun didn't shine — is exactly what ages a roof fastest. Add the densest housing stock in Pinellas County and some of its oldest neighborhoods, and St. Pete roofing becomes a specialist's market. The Historic Kenwood and Old Northeast bungalow districts carry roofs over homes approaching their century mark. The vast mid-century block neighborhoods from Central Avenue south to Pinellas Point carry 60-year-old low-slope sections and additions. And Shore Acres and Snell Isle homeowners know better than anyone in the county what a storm surge season feels like.

Bungalow Districts: Craft Over Speed

A re-roof in Kenwood isn't a production job. Original plank decking, steep pitches, exposed rafter tails, and a neighborhood full of homeowners who care how the street looks — we bring shingle profiles and colors that respect the district, and we repair what we find underneath instead of covering it. Our replacement crews have done enough pre-war St. Pete homes to quote them accurately the first time.

Shore Acres Knows. So Do We.

Some St. Pete neighborhoods live with water in a way the rest of Tampa Bay only reads about. After storm events our storm damage team works Shore Acres, Riviera Bay, and the coastal streets with same-day tarping when conditions allow, full photo documentation, and direct work with carriers — and because Florida's claim-reporting window is limited, we urge storm-area homeowners to get inspected even when the roof "looks fine."

Mid-Century Block Homes: The Quiet Majority

Most of St. Pete lives under a 1950s-70s concrete block ranch, and those roofs have their own patterns: low-slope rear additions that shed water poorly, decades of layered patches, and flashing lines original to the Eisenhower administration. Our repair service traces leaks to the real source, and when replacement time comes, we build the whole system — including the low-slope sections production crews shortcut. City of St. Petersburg permits, pulled and managed by us.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you work in St. Petersburg's historic districts?

Regularly — Historic Kenwood, Old Northeast, Crescent Heights, and the surrounding bungalow neighborhoods. We match materials to the home's era and the district's character, and handle the older decking and framing surprises these homes hide without change-order games.

Can you handle the flat and low-slope sections on my St. Pete home?

Yes. Mid-century St. Pete homes commonly mix shingle field with low-slope or flat additions, and the transition is where they leak. We install proper low-slope membrane systems where pitch requires it, integrated correctly with the shingle roof — not shingles stretched past their rated slope.

How quickly can you respond after a storm hits Shore Acres or Snell Isle?

We run 24/7 storm response with same-day tarping whenever conditions allow, and St. Petersburg is a short run from our Largo headquarters. After major events we prioritize active leaks first, then documentation inspections — both matter for your insurance claim.

Who pulls the roofing permit in St. Petersburg?

We do. St. Pete homes permit through the City of St. Petersburg, and we handle the application, fees, and all required inspections as part of every job.
