Dunedin Cares How Things Look — and Should

Few Florida towns have Dunedin's kind of streetscape: the walkable Main Street core, golf carts on brick side streets, cottages and bungalows along Edgewater Drive and the Pinellas Trail, all of it with an old-Florida-meets-Scottish-festival character homeowners actively protect. Roofing here is a visible act. A poorly chosen color or a cheap-looking profile changes a street; a well-matched architectural shingle disappears into the town's fabric the way it should. We treat that as part of the job, not an afterthought — color and profile consultation is built into every Dunedin replacement estimate.

Coastal Reality Behind the Charm

Dunedin fronts the Gulf across from Honeymoon and Caladesi islands, and its weather exposure is genuinely coastal: salt-influenced air, onshore wind, and storm systems that arrive with open-water momentum. The cottages that give the town its charm are also some of its oldest roofs. Our installations pair the curb-appeal conversation with coastal substance — corrosion-appropriate components, enhanced wind nailing, and complete Atlas Signature Select systems that keep the manufacturer warranty whole.

Small Homes, Detailed Work

A 1950s Dunedin cottage roof is a small job by square footage and a detailed one by nature: aged decking, additions with slope transitions, mature-tree debris, and character elements worth protecting. Our repair crews do this work constantly — and our gutter, soffit, and fascia service keeps rooflines crisp on streets where trim detail is half the look.

Local Enough to Bump Into

Our Largo headquarters is minutes down the Pinellas Trail corridor, which means Dunedin estimates, repairs, and emergency calls get neighbor-grade response. Free written estimates, City of Dunedin permits handled, background-checked crews, and a magnetic nail sweep before we leave — golf-cart tires and bare feet are non-negotiable constituencies here.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you help choose a shingle color that fits my Dunedin neighborhood?

Yes — it's a standard part of our estimate visit. We bring samples, look at your home's exterior palette and the surrounding streetscape, and recommend profiles and colors that fit the block. Architectural shingles offer far better options for this than older 3-tab looks.

Do you work on the older cottages near downtown Dunedin?

All the time. Pre-1960s cottages have their own patterns — plank decking, framing quirks, slope transitions on additions — and we quote them from experience rather than discovering them mid-job at your expense.

Is Dunedin's coastal exposure really different from inland Pinellas?

Measurably. Onshore Gulf wind and salt-influenced air age fasteners and flashing faster and demand better wind detailing. It's the difference between a roof spec'd for a map and one spec'd for where you actually live — ours are the latter.

Do you pull City of Dunedin permits?

Yes. Dunedin permits roofing work through the city, and we manage the full process — application through final inspection — on every project.
