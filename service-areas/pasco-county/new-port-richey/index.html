The Oldest Roof Stock on Pasco's Coast

New Port Richey grew up around the Cotee River a century ago, and the neighborhoods near Sims Park and the downtown historic district still carry homes from that founding era — with all the plank decking, steep pitches, and craftsmanship detail that implies. Around that core, the 1970s-80s boom filled the map with concrete-block neighborhoods now on second or third roofs. This is repair-and-restoration country as much as replacement country, and our crews work it accordingly: preserve what's sound, fix what's failed, and never sell a tear-off where a proper repair serves the home better.

Gulf Harbors: Salt Changes the Spec

West of US-19, Gulf Harbors puts homes directly on saltwater canals with open Gulf exposure beyond. Salt air is quietly brutal on the metal parts of a roof — fasteners, drip edge, valley metal, vent flashing — and canal-front roofs routinely show corroded components under shingles that still look presentable. Every Gulf Harbors quote we write specs corrosion-resistant components and wind-appropriate installation as the baseline, because inland spec on a canal home is a roof built to fail early.

Storm Response for the River and the Coast

Gulf-side Pasco catches coastal storm systems with little in the way. When weather hits New Port Richey, our storm damage team brings same-day tarping when conditions allow, complete photo documentation, and direct coordination with your insurance carrier — including meeting the adjuster on your roof. With Florida's claim window at one year, post-storm inspections here are worth doing even when nothing looks wrong from the yard.

Permits: City or County, Sorted

Addresses inside city limits permit through the City of New Port Richey; surrounding areas run through Pasco County. We handle both routinely, along with everything else between the free written estimate and the magnetic nail sweep at cleanup. Our replacements carry a lifetime workmanship warranty backed by a company that's been here since 2010.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you restore the roof on an older home near downtown New Port Richey?

Yes — the historic core around Sims Park is familiar territory. Century-old homes need era-appropriate judgment: matching profiles and colors, repairing plank decking properly, and handling steep pitches and detail work production crews avoid. We quote these from experience.

Why do Gulf Harbors roofs wear out faster?

Salt air corrodes the metal components — fasteners, flashing, drip edge — that hold a roof together, and open-water wind exposure stresses every sealant bond. The shingles often outlast the hardware. Coastal-spec components and installation, which we include by default in Gulf Harbors quotes, close that gap.

Do I permit through the city or Pasco County?

Inside New Port Richey city limits, the city; outside, the county. Either way it's our job, not yours — we pull the permit and manage every required inspection.

How fast can you tarp a leaking roof in New Port Richey?

We run 24/7 emergency response across Pasco County with same-day tarping whenever conditions allow. Call (813) 590-9050 any hour — fast temporary protection limits interior damage and strengthens any insurance claim that follows.
