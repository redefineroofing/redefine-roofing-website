What Hillsborough County Roofs Are Up Against

Tampa is one of the lightning and thunderstorm capitals of the United States, and Hillsborough roofs absorb the consequences: near-daily summer downpours, damaging straight-line winds, occasional hail, and the outer bands — or direct hits — of tropical systems moving up the Gulf. Add year-round UV intensity and humidity, and shingle roofs here age measurably faster than national averages suggest.

Homes along the bay in South Tampa and Apollo Beach deal with the extra corrosion pressure of salt air, while older inland neighborhoods like Seminole Heights, Temple Terrace, and central Brandon have large numbers of roofs installed 15–25 years ago that are now reaching the end of their service life — often right as insurance carriers start asking hard questions about roof age at renewal.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you serve all of Hillsborough County?

Yes — Tampa, Brandon, Riverview, Valrico, Plant City, Lutz, Carrollwood, Westchase, Temple Terrace, Apollo Beach, Wimauma, and everywhere in between. If you're in Hillsborough County, you're in our service area.

Do I need a permit to replace a roof in Hillsborough County?

Yes. Roof replacements in Hillsborough County require a permit, issued by the county or by your municipality (such as the City of Tampa, Temple Terrace, or Plant City). As your licensed contractor, we pull the permit and schedule all required inspections for you.

How fast can you respond to storm damage in Tampa?

We offer 24/7 emergency response across Hillsborough County with same-day tarping whenever conditions allow. Being based in the Tampa Bay area means our crews are minutes away, not mobilizing from out of state.
