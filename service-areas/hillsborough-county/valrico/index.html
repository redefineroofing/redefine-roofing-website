The Trees Are the Neighborhood — and the Problem

Drive through Bloomingdale East, Buckhorn, or River Hills and the mature oak canopy is the first thing you notice. Valrico homeowners love their trees, and they should. But every roofer who works here knows the flip side: dropped limbs that crack shingles and punch decking, acorns and leaf litter that pack valleys and gutters, and deep shade that keeps shingles damp — the perfect recipe for the black algae streaks and moss that age a roof ahead of its time.

We're not going to tell you to cut down your oaks. We're going to build and maintain a roof that lives with them.

Repairs Are Half Our Valrico Work

Limb impact damage is usually localized — a cracked shingle field here, a bent flashing line there. Our repair crews fix the damage properly (including the decking underneath when a big limb hits), match your existing shingles, and back it with our workmanship warranty. No job too small, and no upsell to a replacement you don't need.

Gutters, Soffit & Fascia Earn Their Keep Here

Under canopy, gutters clog faster and overflow more, and overflowing gutters rot fascia — we see the sequence constantly in Valrico's 1980s-90s housing stock. Our gutter, soffit, and fascia work replaces rotted wood properly and sizes drainage for both Florida rain volume and Valrico leaf load.

Frequently Asked Questions

A tree limb just hit my roof in Valrico. What should I do first?

If there's an opening or active leak, call us at (813) 590-9050 — we offer 24/7 response and same-day tarping when conditions allow. Photograph the damage from the ground, and don't climb up yourself. Limb strikes often damage decking beneath the shingles, so a proper assessment matters even when the surface looks minor.

Are the black streaks on my Valrico roof damaging it?

Those streaks are algae (gloeocapsa magma), which thrives in Valrico's shade and humidity. The streaks themselves are mostly cosmetic, but the same damp conditions that grow algae also grow moss, which does hold moisture against shingles and shortens their life. When we replace roofs here, we use algae-resistant shingles that fight the problem at the source.

Does homeowners insurance cover tree damage to my roof?

Generally yes — sudden tree or limb damage from wind is a covered peril under most policies, including resulting interior damage. We document the damage, provide the repair scope, and work directly with your carrier. What's not covered is long-term wear from debris and shade, which is a maintenance conversation we'll have honestly.

Do you work throughout Valrico?

Yes — Bloomingdale East, Buckhorn, River Hills, Twin Lakes, and everywhere across Valrico and neighboring Brandon, Lithia, and Seffner.
