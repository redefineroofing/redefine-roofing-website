A City Where No Two Roofs Are the Same Job

Tampa isn't one housing market — it's five or six stacked on top of each other. A craftsman bungalow in Seminole Heights or Hyde Park has steep pitches, aging decking, and character worth preserving. A mid-century block ranch in South Tampa off Bayshore has low slopes and sixty years of patch history. A two-story in Westchase or New Tampa has a complex modern roofline and an HOA with opinions. Treating those as the same job is how roofs get done wrong, and Tampa homeowners can usually tell stories about a roofer who tried.

Redefine Roofing has replaced and repaired roofs across every one of those neighborhoods since 2010. We're licensed and insured (CCC 1331457), Atlas and CertainTeed certified, and our estimates are free, written, and line-itemed.

Older Tampa Homes: What We Watch For

In Tampa's historic districts, the roof you see is rarely the whole story. Original plank decking, layered tear-offs from decades past, and framing that predates modern code all live under those shingles. Our crews open up, document, and fix what's underneath instead of nailing new material over old problems — and we match shingle profiles and colors so a new roof doesn't fight a 100-year-old street's character. When a targeted repair will honestly buy years, we say so.

Storm Season in the Lightning Capital

Tampa Bay records more lightning and severe afternoon thunderstorms than almost anywhere in the country, and the wind-driven rain that comes with them finds every weak flashing line and lifted shingle in the city. After storms cross Tampa, our storm damage team runs documented inspections, same-day tarping when conditions allow, and works directly with your insurance company.

Permits, Handled — City or County

Inside Tampa city limits, roofing permits run through the City of Tampa, not Hillsborough County — a distinction that trips up out-of-area contractors. We pull the correct permit, schedule the inspections, and build every replacement to exceed Florida Building Code wind requirements. If your attic runs hot — common in Tampa's older housing stock — we'll assess ventilation and insulation in the same visit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you handle City of Tampa roofing permits?

Yes. Homes inside Tampa city limits permit through the City of Tampa rather than Hillsborough County, and we manage that process — application, fees, and inspections — as part of every job. Homes in unincorporated areas like Westchase go through the county, and we handle those too.

Can you replace a roof on a historic bungalow without ruining its character?

That's a specialty of ours. We match shingle profile and color to the neighborhood, preserve or replicate original detail work at eaves and gables, and repair the older decking underneath properly. Seminole Heights, Hyde Park, and Ybor-area homes make up a meaningful share of our Tampa work.

Which Tampa neighborhoods do you serve?

All of them — South Tampa, Seminole Heights, Hyde Park, Ybor City, Westchase, Carrollwood, Town 'N' Country, Temple Terrace, New Tampa, and everywhere between. We've completed projects across the city since 2010.

How fast can you get to a Tampa roof leak?

We offer 24/7 emergency response with same-day tarping whenever conditions allow. Tampa is minutes from our Largo headquarters across the bay, so emergency calls get real response times, not next-week appointments.
