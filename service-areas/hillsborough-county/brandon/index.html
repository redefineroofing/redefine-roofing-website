Brandon's Roofs Are All Hitting the Same Birthday

Brandon's big growth wave came in the 1970s through the 1990s — the ranches and two-stories of Bloomingdale, Providence Lakes, Limona, and the neighborhoods off the SR 60 corridor went up in the same few decades. Do the math on a 20-25 year shingle lifespan and you get today's reality: a huge share of Brandon homes are either on their second roof already or living on borrowed time with their first.

That age wave has consequences beyond leaks. Florida insurance carriers now ask hard questions about roof age at every renewal, and a 18-year-old roof — even a healthy one — can complicate your coverage. Knowing exactly where your roof stands, in writing, has real financial value here.

Start With the Truth: A Free, Documented Inspection

Our roof inspections are free and end with a written, photographed report: remaining service life, items that need attention now, and items that can wait. If your roof has years left, the report says so — useful leverage at insurance renewal. If it's time, you'll see the evidence yourself, not take a salesman's word for it. That education-first approach is why Brandon neighbors refer us down the street.

When It's Time: Replacement Done Right

Our roof replacements are complete systems — tear-off, deck inspection and repair, underlayment, flashing, ventilation, and Atlas Signature Select shingles installed to exceed code wind requirements. Most Brandon homes take one to three days. Permits run through unincorporated Hillsborough County, and we handle the entire process.

Storm Damage Doesn't Wait for Roof Age

Brandon's inland position spares it salt air but not the violent summer thunderstorm cells that track across east Hillsborough with straight-line wind and hail. A five-year-old roof can be storm-totaled just like a twenty-year-old one. If weather has crossed your neighborhood, our storm and insurance claim team documents everything and meets your adjuster on the roof.

Frequently Asked Questions

My Brandon home was built in 1988. Should I be planning for a roof?

If the roof hasn't been replaced since around 2005, yes — you're in the planning window even without visible problems. A free documented inspection tells you whether that's a this-year project or a three-years-from-now project, and gives you a written report your insurance carrier will take seriously.

Will an aging roof affect my homeowners insurance in Brandon?

Increasingly, yes. Florida carriers routinely require roof condition documentation on roofs past 10-15 years and may non-renew policies over roof age alone. An inspection report showing remaining service life — or a replacement, when it's genuinely time — is often the difference at renewal.

How much does a roof replacement cost for a typical Brandon home?

It depends on the home's size, pitch, and decking condition, so we quote from a free on-site inspection rather than a formula. Every quote is written and line-itemed with no hidden fees — and because our team is paid to educate rather than hard-sell, you'll never get a replacement pitch when a repair would do.

Do you serve the neighborhoods around Brandon too?

Yes — Bloomingdale, Providence Lakes, Limona, and neighboring Valrico, Riverview, and Seffner. If you're anywhere in the greater Brandon area, you're squarely inside our service territory.
