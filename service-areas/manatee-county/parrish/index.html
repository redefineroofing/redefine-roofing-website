New-Build Owners: Establish Your Baseline

Most Parrish homes are young, and young production roofs benefit from one smart move: a free documented baseline inspection. It catches builder shortcuts while they're still warranty conversations, gives you dated photos that make any future storm claim dramatically cleaner, and starts a written condition record that pays off at every insurance renewal. It costs nothing and takes an afternoon.

Homesteads: The Whole Property, One Visit

One free visit covers everything with a roof on it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you actually service Parrish, or is it the edge of your map?

Full service, not edge-of-map — Parrish is squarely inside the Manatee County territory we've covered since 2010. Estimates, repairs, replacements, and emergency response all run at our normal standards and timelines here.

We're in North River Ranch. Do you handle our HOA requirements?

Yes — architectural approval paperwork, color samples, and community rules are handled as part of any project that needs them, in North River Ranch and the corridor's other new communities.

Can you roof my barn and workshop as well as the house?

Yes — metal and shingle roofing for outbuildings is regular Parrish work. We quote the whole property in one free visit so you can prioritize and plan the pieces on your budget.

Why would a brand-new Parrish roof need an inspection?

To document it. A dated, photographed baseline catches production-build shortcuts early and becomes powerful evidence if a storm ever damages the roof — separating new damage from pre-existing condition is the heart of every insurance claim, and a baseline makes it easy.
