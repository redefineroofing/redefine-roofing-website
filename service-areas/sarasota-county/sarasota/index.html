A City Where Architecture Is the Point

Sarasota takes its buildings seriously — this is the city of the Sarasota School of Architecture, the Ringling legacy, and neighborhoods where mid-century modern isn't a trend but an inheritance. From Arlington Park and Gulf Gate's 1960s-70s ranches to the bayfront's contemporary showpieces, Sarasota homes wear their rooflines like tailoring. Our job is to keep the performance side — Gulf-coast wind, rain, sun — from ever compromising the design side, and vice versa.

Low-Slope Is a Specialty, Not an Afterthought

Mid-century Sarasota means low-slope and flat sections: dramatic horizontal rooflines that define the architecture and defeat production shingle crews. Shingles stretched below their rated slope fail early — the classic Sarasota mistake. We install proper low-slope membrane systems where the pitch demands them, integrated cleanly with shingle fields where the roof transitions, so the water management works as well as the sightlines. Our repair crews untangle decades of previous shortcuts on these roofs weekly.

Coastal Performance Under the Aesthetics

Bayfront and barrier-adjacent Sarasota lives with real coastal exposure — salt-influenced air and open-water wind that recent hurricane seasons have driven home countywide. Every replacement we build here pairs the design conversation (profiles, colors, sightlines) with coastal substance: corrosion-appropriate components, enhanced wind nailing, complete Atlas Signature Select systems installed to exceed Florida code. City of Sarasota permits handled by us.

Documented Condition for a Demanding Market

Sarasota's active real-estate and insurance markets both run on documentation. Our free inspections produce written, photographed reports buyers, sellers, and carriers all accept — and after storms, our claims team documents damage while it's attributable and works your carrier directly, adjuster meeting included. Florida's one-year claim window makes the prompt inspection the smart default.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you roof a mid-century home without wrecking its lines?

That's precisely our Sarasota specialty. Low-slope sections get proper membrane systems rather than out-of-spec shingles, transitions are detailed cleanly, and material profiles are chosen to preserve the horizontal aesthetic these homes are built around. The architecture is why you bought the house; the roof should honor it.

Do you work on Sarasota's bayfront and near the barrier islands?

Yes — bayfront neighborhoods and barrier-adjacent streets are regular territory, quoted with coastal-spec components and wind detailing appropriate to open-water exposure.

What does a roof inspection cover for a Sarasota home sale?

The complete system: surface materials, flashing, penetrations, low-slope sections, visible decking, and attic ventilation, all documented in a written report with photos and a remaining-life assessment. It's free, and it reads the same to buyers, sellers, and insurance carriers.

Who issues roofing permits in Sarasota?

Inside city limits, the City of Sarasota; beyond, Sarasota County. We pull and manage both routinely, inspections included.
