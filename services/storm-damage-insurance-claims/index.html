After the Storm, You Need a Local Team You Can Trust

Tampa Bay homeowners know the drill: hurricanes and tropical systems in summer and fall, violent thunderstorms with damaging wind and hail the rest of the year. When your roof takes a hit, two things matter — protecting your home immediately, and documenting the damage properly so your insurance claim reflects what actually happened.

Redefine Roofing has restored storm-damaged homes across Hillsborough, Pinellas, Pasco, Manatee, Sarasota, and Hernando counties since 2010. We're local, licensed (CCC 1331457), and we'll still be here long after the out-of-state storm chasers have moved on to the next disaster zone.

Our Storm Response Process

  1. Emergency stabilization. Call us 24/7 and we'll get your roof tarped — same-day whenever conditions allow — to stop water intrusion and prevent secondary damage to ceilings, walls, and flooring.
  2. Thorough damage documentation. We photograph and document every impact point, lifted shingle, and compromised component. This documentation becomes the factual record your claim is built on.
  3. We work directly with your insurance company. We provide our documentation to your carrier, meet your adjuster on the roof to make sure nothing gets missed, and answer their technical questions so you don't have to.
  4. Full restoration. Once your claim is approved, we restore your roof to pre-storm condition — or better — using manufacturer-certified installation and materials rated to exceed local code.

Know Your Rights (and Your Deadlines)

Florida law currently gives homeowners a limited window — generally one year from the date of loss — to report a new property insurance claim. Waiting until you notice a ceiling stain months later can put your claim at risk. If a storm has passed over your home, a free post-storm inspection is the smart move even if everything looks fine from the ground: hail bruising and sealant-line wind damage are nearly invisible without getting on the roof.

One more thing every Florida homeowner should know: your deductible is your responsibility by law — any contractor offering to "waive" or "cover" your deductible is committing insurance fraud and putting you at legal risk. We'll never play those games, and you should walk away from anyone who does.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do immediately after storm damage?

First, stay off the roof — storm-damaged roofs are dangerous. Take photos of any visible damage and interior leaks from the ground and inside your home, move belongings away from drips, and call us at (813) 590-9050 for emergency tarping. Prompt temporary protection prevents secondary damage and shows your insurer you acted responsibly.

How long do I have to file a hurricane or storm claim in Florida?

Under current Florida law, you generally have one year from the date of loss to report a new claim, and 18 months for a supplemental claim. Don't wait — hidden damage is easiest to document and tie to a specific storm right after it happens.

Will my insurance cover my roof replacement?

It depends on your policy and the damage. Insurance covers sudden storm damage, not wear and tear — and some policies pay only actual cash value on older roofs. Our free inspection documents exactly what the storm did, giving you and your carrier a factual basis for the claim decision.

Do you handle the insurance claim for me?

We work directly with your insurance company: we provide complete damage documentation, meet your adjuster on-site, and answer technical questions about scope and repair methods. The claim itself remains yours — Florida law is strict about who can negotiate claims — but you won't be navigating the technical side alone.

What does emergency tarping cost?

Emergency tarping is often covered by your insurance claim as reasonable protection against further damage. Call us 24/7 and we'll assess the situation, get your roof protected — same-day whenever possible — and document everything for your claim.
