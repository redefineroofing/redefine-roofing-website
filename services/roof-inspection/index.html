Peace of Mind Starts With the Facts

Your roof does its job silently until the day it doesn't. A professional inspection catches problems while they're still small and inexpensive — and in Florida, where roof age and condition directly affect your ability to get and keep homeowners insurance, knowing your roof's real condition isn't optional anymore. Redefine Roofing has inspected and documented thousands of Tampa Bay roofs since 2010, and every inspection ends with a clear, photographed written report — not a sales pitch.

When You Need a Roof Inspection

  • Before buying a home. A general home inspector looks at the roof from a ladder for a few minutes. A roofing professional gets on the roof and tells you what you're really buying — and what it will cost you in the next five years.
  • Before selling. Roof condition is one of the first things buyers' inspectors and insurers flag in Florida. Knowing about issues before listing lets you fix them on your terms, not during a contract negotiation.
  • After severe weather. Wind and hail damage are often invisible from the ground. A post-storm inspection documents damage while it's still clearly attributable to the storm — critical, since Florida's claim-filing window is limited.
  • At insurance renewal time. Carriers increasingly require roof condition documentation on older roofs. An inspection report showing remaining service life can make the difference at renewal.
  • Routine checkups. For roofs past their tenth year, a periodic inspection is cheap insurance against expensive surprises.

What We Inspect

Our inspections cover the complete roofing system, not just the shingles: surface materials and granule condition, flashing at every penetration and wall intersection, pipe boots and vents, drip edge, gutters, soffit, and fascia, visible decking condition, attic ventilation, and interior warning signs like stains or moisture where accessible. You get a written report with photos of everything we find, an honest assessment of remaining service life, and prioritized recommendations — what needs attention now, what can wait, and what's just cosmetic.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I have my roof inspected in Florida?

Once a year is ideal for roofs over ten years old, and always after a major storm passes over your area. Florida's sun, rain, and wind age roofs faster than in most of the country, so problems develop on a shorter timeline here.

What does a roof inspection cost?

Redefine Roofing offers free roof inspections with a detailed written report and photos. There's no obligation and no pressure — if the roof is healthy, the report says so.

Is a roofing inspection different from a wind mitigation inspection?

Yes. A wind mitigation inspection is a specific insurance form documenting wind-resistant features for premium credits, typically performed by a licensed inspector. Our roof inspection assesses condition, damage, and remaining life. Many homeowners use both: ours to know what the roof needs, wind mitigation to capture insurance discounts after improvements.

Can an inspection help with my insurance claim?

Absolutely. Post-storm inspection photos and written documentation establish what damage exists and tie it to a specific weather event — the foundation of a solid claim. We document everything we find and work directly with your insurance company if you decide to file.

Do I need an inspection before buying a home in Tampa Bay?

We strongly recommend it. Roof replacement is one of the largest costs a new homeowner can face, and roof condition affects your insurance options immediately at closing. A dedicated roof inspection tells you the roof's true remaining life before you commit — powerful information in any negotiation.
